About Us

We choose not to introduce ourselves. While our platform contributes positively to societal progress, it inevitably challenges certain interests, and human nature can be unpredictable. To avoid retaliation from those whose interests are affected, we have decided to remain anonymous.

Our Mission

Academic fraud and plagiarism are harmful to the integrity of the scholarly community, undermining trust, progress, and the value of genuine research. However, exposing and addressing these issues has become increasingly challenging. For instance, platforms like arXiv no longer allow commentary, and some journals refuse to accept critiques of papers they have published, limiting the space for honest discourse.

In fact, several of the comments published on our site were previously submitted to journals, but editors rejected them, stating that they only accept papers, not critiques. As a result, honest researchers are finding it harder and harder to voice their concerns or challenge unethical practices.

This platform was created to address this gap. Our goal is to provide a space where academic misconduct can be revealed and discussed, fostering a healthier and more ethical academic community. Together, we aim to uphold the values of transparency, integrity, and accountability in research.

We welcome contributions, insights, and discussions from researchers and academics who share our commitment to integrity and accountability. Our platform operates without bias, with the sole purpose of uncovering the truth and promoting ethical practices in research. We also strive to ensure that all discussions remain professional, evidence-based, and respectful.
