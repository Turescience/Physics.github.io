Comments on “Phase Transitions and Critical Phenomena for the FRW Universe in an Effective Scalar Tensor Theory (Phys. Dark Univ., 2023, 42, 101330)”

Abstract

This paper reviews the "Phase Transitions and Critical Phenomena for the FRW Universe in an Effective Scalar Tensor Theory (Phys. Dark Univ., 2023, 42, 101330)." We found that the title of the paper contains serious errors. Moreover, the paper lacks any form of innovation.

Comments

Recently, we published a paper titled "Paper Mills and Research Integrity in Cosmology: Investigating P-V Phase Transitions in Massive Systems" [1]. The current paper builds upon and extends the findings from that earlier work.

As highlighted in [1], the field of cosmological thermodynamics faces significant challenges, including issues of academic misconduct such as fraud, duplicate publications, and plagiarism. The paper under review, titled Phase Transitions and Critical Phenomena for the FRW Universe in an Effective Scalar-Tensor Theory [2], also contains notable errors.

The title of the first paper is grammatically incorrect and poorly phrased. It seems the authors intended to express something like "Thermodynamic Phase Transitions and Critical Phenomena in the FRW Universe within a Scalar-Tensor Theory." However, the second paper does not even use Scalar-Tensor Theory; it merely applies results derived from such a framework. There is no need to emphasize this in the title. A more accurate title would be "Phase Transitions and Critical Phenomena in the FRW Universe under Lovelock Gravity."

Beyond these issues, this paper is essentially identical to your other paper titled "The P-V phase transition of the FRW universe" [3], with the only difference being the inclusion of figures. Specifically, the figures in this paper are merely visual representations of the equations from the earlier work. This constitutes clear duplicate publication.
